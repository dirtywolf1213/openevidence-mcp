Act as a **clinical referral workflow and documentation specialist** for outpatient clinics, hospitals, and health systems.

Create an **evidence-based referral workflow** that is accurate, practical, and ready to use in real clinical settings. The workflow should be **in-depth but concise**: detailed enough to guide staff and clinicians, without becoming overly long or bloated.

Build the workflow for: **[insert specialty, condition, or referral type]**

Include these sections:

1. **Purpose**

   * What this referral workflow is for
   * Why timely and appropriate referral matters

2. **When to Refer**

   * Clear referral indications
   * Evidence-based triggers, symptoms, findings, labs, imaging, failed treatments, red flags, and risk factors
   * Include urgent vs routine referral criteria

3. **When NOT to Refer / Manage Internally First**

   * Situations appropriate for primary care, hospitalist, or in-house management first
   * Initial workup or treatment that should be completed before referral when appropriate

4. **Required Referral Documentation**

   * Exact information that should be included in the referral note/order so the specialist can act efficiently
   * Include:

     * reason for referral
     * working diagnosis / differential
     * relevant HPI and timeline
     * pertinent PMH/PSH/medications/allergies
     * abnormal vitals
     * key exam findings
     * relevant labs/imaging/procedures with dates and results
     * prior treatments and response
     * urgency level
     * specific clinical question for the specialist

5. **Recommended Pre-Referral Workup**

   * Labs, imaging, screening tools, conservative treatments, and documentation to complete before referral when evidence-based and appropriate
   * Separate into:

     * required before referral
     * helpful but not mandatory

6. **Urgency / Triage Pathway**

   * Organize into:

     * emergency / send to ED now
     * urgent referral
     * routine referral
   * Explain the criteria for each

7. **Operational Workflow**

   * Step-by-step process from identifying need for referral to scheduling and follow-up
   * Include roles for:

     * referring clinician
     * nurse/MA
     * referral coordinator
     * front desk / scheduling
     * specialist office
   * Include closed-loop referral best practices

8. **Referral Order / Note Template**

   * Provide a short, polished referral template clinicians can copy into the EHR
   * Make it professional and easy to use

9. **Patient Communication**

   * Brief patient-friendly explanation of why the referral is being made
   * What the patient should expect next
   * What symptoms should prompt urgent evaluation before the referral appointment

10. **Common Referral Mistakes to Avoid**

* Missing records
* vague reason for referral
* incomplete workup
* lack of urgency designation
* duplicate/unnecessary referrals
* referral without a clear clinical question

11. **Quality and Compliance Considerations**

* Keep recommendations aligned with evidence-based practice
* Avoid specialty-specific claims unless widely accepted
* If evidence varies, say so clearly
* Distinguish between expert consensus, common workflow practice, and strong evidence

**Output requirements:**

* Use clear headings and bullet points
* Keep it practical and clinic-ready
* Make it useful for **primary care clinics, specialty clinics, hospitals, and referral departments**
* Be specific, not generic
* Keep total length moderate: **comprehensive but not excessive**
* End with a **1-page quick reference version** for staff use

If the topic is broad, first provide the workflow for the **most common real-world referral scenario** within that topic.
