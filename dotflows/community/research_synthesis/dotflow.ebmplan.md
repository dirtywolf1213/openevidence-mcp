Generate an evidence based plan for the clinical issue. Make recomendations based on evidence from guidelines, clinical trials, and rational clinical exams.

See example of format below:

# Suspected new-onset acute decompensated heart failure (ADHF) with volume overload
Presentation (progressive dyspnea including at rest, orthopnea/PND, weight gain, bilateral edema) plus CXR findings (cardiomegaly, pulmonary congestion/edema, pleural effusions) is most consistent with ADHF; need to characterize EF and evaluate for ischemic and other precipitants, and initiate diuresis with careful renal/electrolyte monitoring. BNP interpretation should account for CKD, as reduced eGFR shifts diagnostic cutpoints upward.

Dx.
12-lead EKG now (evaluate for ischemia/arrhythmia).
BNP or NT-proBNP on admission to support diagnosis/risk stratification (interpret in context of CKD).
Transthoracic echocardiogram with Doppler to assess EF, chamber size, wall motion, and valve disease.
Chest radiograph already obtained; no routine daily CXR unless clinical change.
Initial HF labs: CBC, CMP/BMP with Mg/Ca, LFTs, TSH, fasting lipid profile, A1c; urinalysis already available and notable for proteinuria/glucosuria.
Telemetry monitoring given reported palpitations.
Troponin x1–3 (trend if elevated and/or symptoms/ECG changes) to assess for myocardial injury/ischemia (precipitant).
Consider ischemic evaluation pathway after initial stabilization if echo/ECG/troponin suggest CAD (patient has multiple CAD risk factors and family history).

Tx.
Diuresis: start furosemide 40 mg IV now; reassess urine output and symptoms, and redose/titrate (e.g., 40 mg IV BID) to goal net negative ~1–2 L/day as tolerated.
Strict I/Os, daily weights, sodium restriction (e.g., 2 g/day).
Oxygen PRN to maintain adequate saturation (target per institutional protocol).
Avoid NSAIDs and other nephrotoxins.
If markedly hypertensive and congested, treat HTN while avoiding hypotension (agent selection guided by vitals and renal function).
Cardiology consult if severe LV dysfunction, refractory congestion, concern for ischemia, significant valvular disease, or complex arrhythmia.
