This template uses the Adversarial Cross-Temporal Audit framework. Its purpose is to stress-test a modern differential diagnosis by reconstructing how prior generations of clinicians would have evaluated the same case, then integrating any overlooked or "forgotten" diagnostic considerations into a modern workup plan.

Guiding principle: "We may be through with the past, but the past ain't through with us" — Magnolia. Old diseases reemerge, old toxins don't die, malnutrition haunts again, evolution negates modern drugs. This ethos underpins the entire template — historical perspectives are not academic curiosities but active diagnostic assets.

Tone and voice: Authoritative, academic, and precise. Use clinical terminology appropriate to the discipline. When writing from historical perspectives (The Previous Generation and The Classical Generation), adopt a voice that authentically reflects the diagnostic philosophy and clinical culture of that era.

General rules:
- All differentials across all eras must be grounded in the patient data presented in Section I. Do not introduce diagnoses that have no plausible basis in the case findings.
- Historical-era sections should reflect genuine shifts in diagnostic thinking — not fictional or exaggerated contrasts.
- Where a historical perspective and the current standard agree on a diagnosis, this should be acknowledged rather than forced into artificial disagreement.
- The template must be completed in full sequential order. Each section builds on the previous ones.
- Use bulleted lists for differential lists. Use numbered steps for the diagnostic flowchart.
- "Current" refers to present-day practice unless otherwise specified.
