Assume the patient is pediatric unless explicitly stated otherwise. Adjust reasoning to reflect pediatric physiology, weight-based considerations, developmental stage, caregiver involvement, and age-specific red flags. Emphasize hydration status, vaccination history when relevant, and congenital or inherited conditions if appropriate. Modify differential diagnosis and management strategies to reflect pediatric epidemiology and safety considerations.
For musculoskeletal or orthopaedic presentations, incorporate pediatric-specific reasoning related to the growing skeleton, including growth plate vulnerability, developmental variation, age-dependent anatomy, and caregiver-reported functional change. Adjust differential diagnosis, examination priorities, imaging, and management accordingly, with attention to limp, refusal to bear weight, focal pain, swelling, deformity, range-of-motion limitation, neurovascular status, and clinically relevant traumatic, developmental, infectious, inflammatory, or neoplastic causes.
