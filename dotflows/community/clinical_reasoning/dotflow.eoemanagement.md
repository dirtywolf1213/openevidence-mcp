SECTION 3: DIETARY ELIMINATION THERAPY

========================================================================

--- INDICATIONS ---

- Recommended by ACG 2025 as an alternative to pharmacologic therapy for EoE

- Appropriate for:

- Patients who prefer non-pharmacologic approach

- Patients with mild-to-moderate disease

- Patients intolerant or nonresponsive to STC/dupilumab

- Pediatric patients (especially with concurrent food allergies)

- Patients motivated to identify and eliminate specific food triggers

- Requires: patient/family commitment, access to dietitian, willingness to undergo serial endoscopies

--- DIETARY APPROACHES EFFICACY ---

EMPIRIC ELIMINATION DIETS (preferred first-line dietary approach):

6-Food Elimination Diet (6FED):

- Eliminate: milk/dairy, wheat, egg, soy, nuts/peanuts, fish/shellfish

- Histologic remission (15 eos/hpf): ~61â€“70% (meta-analysis: 61.3%)

- Clinical response: ~93%

- Most studied approach; highest remission rates among empiric diets

- Drawback: highly restrictive; requires multiple endoscopies for food reintroduction

4-Food Elimination Diet (4FED):

- Eliminate: milk/dairy, wheat, egg, soy (or legumes)

- Histologic remission: ~49% (meta-analysis: 49.4%)

- Clinical response: ~74%

- Less restrictive than 6FED with moderate efficacy

2-Food Elimination Diet (2FED):

- Eliminate: milk/dairy and wheat

- Histologic remission: ~42â€“43%

- Fewer endoscopies required; better adherence

- Recommended as starting point in step-up approach

1-Food (Milk) Elimination Diet (1FED):

- Eliminate: milk/dairy only

- Histologic remission: ~34â€“54% (meta-analysis: 51.4%)

- Clinical response: ~87%

- Simplest approach; fewest endoscopies

- RCT data: 1FED vs. 6FED showed similar response rates (34% vs. 40%, P = 0.58)

ELEMENTAL DIET:

- Amino acid-based formula exclusively (no intact protein)

- Histologic remission: ~94% (highest of all dietary approaches)

- Reserved for: infants, severe/refractory EoE, failure of other approaches

- Drawbacks: costly, poor palatability (may require feeding tube), risk of de novo IgE-mediated food allergies on reintroduction

- Requires allergist collaboration for food reintroduction

ALLERGY TEST-DIRECTED ELIMINATION:

- Based on skin prick testing, serum IgE, or patch testing

- Histologic response: ~46â€“51%

- NOT recommended as primary approach (EoE is non-IgE-mediated; testing not standardized)

- May be considered as adjunct in select patients

--- STEP-UP vs. STEP-DOWN APPROACH ---

STEP-UP (preferred per ACG 2025 for efficiency):

Start with 1FED or 2FED â†’ if no response, escalate to 4FED â†’ if no response, escalate to 6FED

- RCT data support similar efficacy of 1FED vs. 6FED upfront

STEP-DOWN (traditional approach):

- Advantages: highest initial remission rate; may achieve deep remission more often

- Disadvantages: most restrictive; most endoscopies required

--- DIETARY THERAPY PATIENT COUNSELING ---

- Dietary therapy is an effective alternative to medications for EoE

- Requires commitment to food avoidance and multiple endoscopies

- Work closely with a registered dietitian experienced in EoE

- Read food labels carefully; hidden allergens (especially milk, wheat) are common

- Dining out requires careful planning and communication with restaurants

- Once food triggers are identified, long-term avoidance maintains remission without medication

- If dietary therapy is too restrictive or ineffective, pharmacologic options remain available

SECTION 4: TREATMENT ALGORITHM SUMMARY

========================================================================

FIRST-LINE OPTIONS (all considered equivalent per ACG 2025; choice via shared decision-making):

1. Topical swallowed corticosteroids (see Section 1)

2. Dietary elimination therapy (see Section 3)

3. PPI therapy (high-dose PPI BID x 8â€“12 weeks; ~50% histologic response)

SECOND-LINE / REFRACTORY:

- If STC failure â†’ consider dupilumab, dietary elimination, or switch STC formulation/dose

- If diet failure â†’ consider STC, dupilumab, or escalate diet (step-up approach)

- If PPI failure â†’ consider STC, dietary elimination, or dupilumab

BIOLOGIC THERAPY:

- Dupilumab (see Section 2): conditionally recommended; particularly for PPI/STC-refractory, moderate-to-severe, or fibrostenotic disease

- Cost considerations: dupilumab significantly more expensive than STC or dietary therapy

COMBINATION THERAPY:

- Concurrent PPI + STC or PPI + dupilumab may be continued if patient was on stable PPI

- Concurrent STC + dupilumab: not studied in clinical trials (STC was prohibited during dupilumab trials)

- Diet + pharmacotherapy: stable elimination diet may be continued with dupilumab

ESOPHAGEAL DILATION:

- Indicated for symptomatic strictures or narrow-caliber esophagus

- Complementary to anti-inflammatory therapy; does not address underlying inflammation

- Should be combined with ongoing medical or dietary therapy
