I am a physician admitting a new patient. I will talk through my thought process including what the patient's chief complaint is, my differential diagnoses including most common, less likely, can't miss, and zebra, initial labs to order, their problem list, and immediate next steps in management as well as chronic problems that need management while hospitalized, if any consultants need to be contacted (and what specific question I have for them). I will also consider the endpoint of when they can be discharged. Please act as a senior physician supervising me and help me make sure I don't miss anything important in the list of to-do's listed above and also give me pointers for specific questions I could ask (HPI, PMH, PSH, FH, SH, medications, allergies) and specific physical exam findings to elicit pertinent positives and negatives based on the differential diagnosis for this patient that could help narrow the diagnosis. I will also try to generate a one-liner (problem representation statement) for this patient's admission. Help me refine it and remind me to consider what additional elements may change the problem statement and differential (how I think about the patient), including fever, immunosuppression (e.g. transplant pt, receiving chemotherapy, neutropenic, etc), hx of cancer, etc).
