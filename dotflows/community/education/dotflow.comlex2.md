Generate 5 COMLEX Level 2–style multiple-choice questions based on the provided content/conversation.
OVERARCHING GOAL:
Questions must simulate real osteopathic clinical reasoning by integrating data gathering, differential diagnosis, and patient-centered management in a biopsychosocial and osteopathic framework.
GENERAL REQUIREMENTS:
•	Each question must be a detailed clinical vignette including:
o	Age, sex, and chief concern
o	Patient perspective (e.g., concern, fear, or goal)
o	Relevant history (medical, surgical, medications, allergies, family, social, sexual when appropriate)
o	Contextual factors (support system, living situation, cultural or psychosocial elements when relevant)
o	Physical exam findings, including osteopathic structural findings when applicable
•	Use realistic labs, imaging, and timelines
COGNITIVE COMPLEXITY:
Ensure a mix:
•	1 first-order (recall)
•	1 second-order (interpretation)
•	2 third-order (clinical application)
•	1 fourth-order (multi-step reasoning with diagnostic + management integration and/or competing diagnoses)
CLINICAL REASONING REQUIREMENTS:
Across the set of questions:
•	Require generation and prioritization of differential diagnoses
•	Include competing diagnoses or “most likely vs most dangerous” scenarios
•	Require interpretation of incomplete or evolving clinical data
CONTENT COVERAGE:
Distribute across questions:
•	Pathophysiology linked to clinical findings
•	Clinical presentation
•	Diagnostic criteria
•	Best initial test
•	Confirmatory testing
•	Next best step in management
•	Preventive care and risk factors
•	Patient counseling or education
•	OMT indications, contraindications, or relevant structural findings
OSTEOPATHIC EMPHASIS:
•	Integrate biomechanical, biopsychosocial, and body–mind–spirit considerations
•	Include OMT or structural exam findings in at least 1–2 questions
•	Incorporate viscerosomatic reflexes or Chapman points when appropriate
PATIENT-CENTERED CARE:
•	Incorporate elements such as:
o	Health literacy
o	Adherence challenges
o	Socioeconomic or cultural considerations affecting care decisions
FORMAT:
SECTION 1: QUESTIONS
•	List all 5 questions
•	Each includes:
o	Clinical vignette
o	Answer choices (A–E)
•	Do NOT include answers or explanations
SECTION 2: ANSWERS AND EXPLANATIONS
For each question:
•	Correct answer
•	Explanation including:
o	Why the correct answer is correct
o	Why each incorrect answer is wrong
o	Clinical reasoning process
•	2–3 key takeaways
STYLE:
•	Match COMLEX Level 2 difficulty
•	Avoid buzzword-only questions
•	Emphasize decision-making over recall
•	Include common traps (e.g., “best initial vs next best step”)
