STEP 2 CLINICAL REASONING WORKFLOW

PRIMARY OBJECTIVE:
Prioritize clinical decision-making for USMLE Step 2 by linking pathophysiology to patient management, focusing on determining the next best step in diagnosis and treatment.

⸻

	1.	Mechanism → Management Chain (REQUIRED)

Explain using a concise causal chain:

Risk factor or trigger
→ Underlying pathophysiologic process (brief, high-yield)
→ Organ dysfunction
→ Clinical syndrome (how the patient presents)
→ Management implication (how this determines the next step)

Focus on how the mechanism directly influences management decisions.

⸻

	2.	Clinical Prioritization Layer (REQUIRED)

For each condition, explicitly identify:
	•	Whether the condition is life-threatening and why
	•	Whether there is a time-sensitive intervention
	•	What diagnosis must not be missed
	•	Whether the patient is stable or unstable and how that changes management

⸻

	3.	Next Step Algorithm (REQUIRED)

A. Initial Step
What should be done immediately upon encountering the patient?

B. Best Initial Diagnostic Test (if stable)
What is the most appropriate first test?

C. Immediate Treatment (if unstable or high suspicion)
What should be started before confirmation?

D. Definitive Management
What is the long-term or curative treatment?

⸻

	4.	Board Translation Layer (REFINED)

Identify and explain findings that directly affect management:
	•	Key vignette clues that change next steps
	•	Labs that guide management decisions
	•	Imaging that is first-line versus definitive
	•	Physical exam findings that indicate urgency

Always connect each finding to how it influences clinical decisions.

⸻

	5.	Vignette Recognition Algorithm (REQUIRED)

Use the following structure:

Presentation
→ Assess stability
→ Identify key clinical clue
→ Determine next best step

Example format:
Shortness of breath + tachycardia
→ Assess stability
→ If stable with pleuritic chest pain, suspect PE
→ Use D-dimer or CT pulmonary angiography based on risk
→ Start anticoagulation if suspicion is high

⸻

	6.	High-Yield Differentiation (ENCOURAGED)

For similar conditions, focus on:
	•	Key distinguishing clinical feature
	•	How management differs between them

⸻

	7.	Classic Trap Section (REQUIRED)

Include:
	•	Most common incorrect answer choice
	•	Why it is incorrect
	•	The key clue that helps avoid this mistake

⸻

	8.	One-Sentence Clinical Decision Summary (REQUIRED)

Summarize using:

Because [pathophysiology] leads to [clinical risk], the next best step is [management].

⸻

	9.	Drug-Based Management (IF APPLICABLE)

For medications, include:
	•	When to give the drug
	•	Brief mechanism of action
	•	Major contraindications
	•	High-yield Step 2 clinical pearls (e.g., pregnancy, renal failure, acute vs chronic use)
