Give patient ready instructions for management of newly diagnosed Eosinophilic Esophagitis.  Include explanation of disease, symptoms, and potential consequences. Include the triad of monitoring: Symptoms, Endoscopic findings, and biopsy results and why just one of these is not enough.  Include the 3 Ds of Eosinophilic Esophagitis treatment including Diet, Drugs, and Dilation.  Make it clear and understandable to patients. Include the following instructions 
The treatment of eosinophilic esophagitis may include medications, food eliminations, and esophageal dilatations as appropriate.  Treatment with esophageal dilatations fails to control the active inflammation.  Treatment with medications or food elimination require repeat endoscopy after 8 weeks to evaluate response to treatment.  Treatment options include proton pump inhibitors twice daily, topical steroids such as Eohilia, Biologics such as dupilumab, and food eliminations and reintroductions.  Most typical food eliminations include cow's milk, with or without elimination of wheat.  
 
Initial treatment will begin with a proton pump inhibitor rabeprazole 20 mg or equivalent taken 30 to 60 minutes before breakfast and 30 to 60 minutes before supper twice daily 
Initial treatment will begin with complete elimination of cows milk in all of its forms including cheeses and dairy and yogurts and ice cream. 
Follow-up in office in 2 to 4 weeks 
Follow up in the office and follow up endoscopy is essential.  To assess whether the treatment is effective, we will need to assess symptoms, repeat endoscopy for findings of rings and narrowing, and repeat biopsies to assess how the eosinophil counts have responded.  The repeat endoscopy should be performed in 6 to 12 weeks.  The treatment needs to be continued diligently and faithfully until the repeat endoscopy is performed.  If the treatment is effective then you will need to stay on the treatment long-term.  If this treatment is ineffective then alternative treatment will be offered. 
Allergy immunology consult is recommended for consideration of immunological therapies such as Dupixent.  Food allergy testing is not effective or recommended.
