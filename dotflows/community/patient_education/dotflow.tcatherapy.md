Let's get you started on a medication called amitriptyline/nortriptyline. This medication is considered a tricyclic antidepressant (TCA) therapy for functional abdominal pain. The evidence shows that TCAs, particularly low-dose amitriptyline, are effective for treating functional abdominal pain and irritable bowel syndrome through their pain-modifying effects rather than their antidepressant properties. The ATLANTIS trial demonstrated that low-dose amitriptyline (10-30 mg daily) is superior to placebo for IBS symptoms in primary care. The American Gastroenterological Association guidelines support the use of TCAs for both IBS with constipation and diarrhea. 
We will start you on 25 mg daily; it can take between 4-6 weeks to see improvement. This medication works differently than pain relievers you might take for a headache. Continue taking it even if you don't feel better right away.
Common side effects:
- Dry mouth - Sip water frequently, chew sugar-free gum, or use sugar-free candy
- Drowsiness or sleepiness - This is why the medication is usually taken at bedtime; avoid driving or operating machinery if you feel drowsy
- Constipation - Increase your water and fiber intake; talk to your doctor if this becomes bothersome
- Blurred vision - Usually temporary; contact your doctor if it persists
- Dizziness when standing up - Stand up slowly from sitting or lying positions
Another rare side effect of this medication is called tardive dyskinesia, which is an involuntary movement disorder that typically affect the face, mouth, tongue, and extremities. That being said, TD is not a high risk associated with tricyclic antidepressants used for functional abdominal pain. Since you are taking a tricyclic antidepressant (like amitriptyline) for your abdominal pain, you do not need to be concerned about developing tardive dyskinesia. This movement disorder only occurs with a different class of medications that work on dopamine receptors in the brain.
Most people that take this medication have very little side effects, especially with the dosage that we prescribe as GI specialists.
