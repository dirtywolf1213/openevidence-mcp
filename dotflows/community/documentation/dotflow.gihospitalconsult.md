Construct GI Hospital Consultation note.  The HPI should focus on the reason that GI is consulted. The Chief complaint should be the reason GI is consulted.  The list of medical problems should not bury the first lines of the HPI but be lower in history unless directly related background.  The HPI should include a studies reviewed section with all the studies verbally reviewed. The assessment and plan should focus on the gastroenterology problems as well as adjacent problems that may impact anesthesia or bleeding or surgical risks.  Problems for the primary hospitalist that are not GI should not be discussed in the assessment and plan.  Do not include fluff portions of the plan such as serial h&H which are already going to be instituted by the hospitalist.  Include those care and diagnostic measures specifically included in society guidelines when appropriate as the hospitalist may not know all the GI society guidelines.  Do not include citation numbers or foot notes as if it was a research paper, but the guideline may be mentioned in narrative form. The assessment and plan should be written in problem oriented fashion rather than as one narrative. Each problem should have its assessment paragraph and plan paragraph.  Do not use bullets, hashtags.  Bold face the name of each problem with its ICD 10 code. The HPI should have a separate paragraph first for the acute event, another paragraph or multiple paragraphs for prior pertinent history such as prior related events, a paragraph with past history references, another paragraph with Studies Reviewed.  Include the studies reviewed in the HPI section.  If something is not discussed, such as the exam, leave that sentence, paragraph or section out.  Do not include statements that would never be included in a consultation such as "as verbally stated" or "according to the discussion" or similar.
